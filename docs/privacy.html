Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

Stepwise (“the app”, “we”, “our”) is a desktop menu-bar application that displays your own health and activity data. This policy explains exactly what data the app accesses, how it is used, and how it is protected. Stepwise is privacy-first by design: your data is read with read-only permission, processed entirely on your own device, and never sent to us or any third party.

Who we are

Stepwise is developed by Dennis Rongo. For any privacy questions, contact contact@leancodeautomation.com.

What data we access

When you choose to connect your Google Account, Stepwise requests the following read-only scopes from the Google Health API. You actively grant these during sign-in and can revoke them at any time (see “Your choices”).

  • Activity & fitness (googlehealth.activity_and_fitness.readonly) — daily and hourly steps, distance, and active minutes.
  • Health metrics & measurements (googlehealth.health_metrics_and_measurements.readonly) — resting heart rate.
  • Sleep (googlehealth.sleep.readonly) — sleep duration.

These scopes are read-only. Stepwise cannot create, modify, or delete any data in your Google Account.

How we use the data

The data is used for a single purpose: to display your steps, the shape of your day, your recent week, and the four supporting metrics inside the app's menu-bar panel on your device. It is fetched on demand, held in memory only while the app renders it, and used for no other purpose.

Where the data goes

  • Directly from Google to your device. The app talks to Google's servers from your own computer. Your health data is never routed through, copied to, or stored on any server operated by us — we operate no servers at all.
  • We do not collect or store your health data. There is no backend, no database, and no analytics. We never see your numbers.
  • No sharing or selling. Your data is never sold, rented, shared, or transferred to any third party, advertiser, or data broker.
  • No advertising or profiling. The app contains no ads and builds no profile of you.

What is stored, and how it's protected

The only credential persisted on your device is the OAuth refresh token Google issues when you connect. It is stored locally, encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a key derived via Argon2id and bound to your machine's identity. It is never written in plaintext and never leaves your device. Sign-in uses a desktop loopback + PKCE flow, so no client secret is exposed in the browser. App settings (such as your goal and theme) are stored as plain local configuration in your operating system's application-data directory.

Limited Use disclosure. Stepwise's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We only use Google user data to provide and improve the in-app features described above, do not transfer it to others except as necessary to provide those features (and never for advertising, profiling, or sale), and do not allow humans to read the data unless we have your consent for specific support, it is required for security or legal reasons, or the data has been aggregated and anonymized.

Data retention and deletion

Because health data is not stored by the app beyond display, there is nothing for us to retain or delete. The locally stored refresh token is removed when you disconnect your account in the app or uninstall Stepwise. You can also revoke Stepwise's access at any time from your Google Account (myaccount.google.com/permissions), which immediately invalidates the token.

Your choices

  • Connecting your Google Account is entirely optional — the app offers a demo mode with placeholder data.
  • Disconnect inside the app to remove the stored token from your device.
  • Revoke access from your Google Account permissions page at any time.

Children's privacy

Stepwise is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected on this page with a new “Last updated” date.

Contact
